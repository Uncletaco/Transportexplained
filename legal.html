2. Vehicle Transport Terms & Conditions

2.1 Scope of Services

OAT provides professional vehicle transport services. Depending on logistics, your shipment may be transported on OAT-owned equipment or, at our discretion, arranged with a qualified, insured motor carrier. Regardless of method, OAT is your primary point of contact throughout the shipment.

2.2 Booking & Pricing

  • Quotes are based on the details you provide and may change if vehicle condition, size, modifications, or pickup/delivery details differ from the original request.
  • Deposit may be required to secure service; deposits are generally non-refundable after a vehicle has been assigned for transport.
  • Balance is due upon delivery unless otherwise agreed in writing. Payment may be made to the driver via approved methods.
  • Non-Payment at delivery may result in storage at your expense until payment is made in full.

2.3 Customer Responsibilities

  • Provide accurate vehicle and contact information.
  • Ensure the vehicle is operable unless noted as inoperable during booking; provide keys.
  • Remove personal items; secure or remove loose parts, antennas, spoilers, covers, and toll devices; disable alarms.
  • Be present (or provide an authorized agent) at pickup and delivery to inspect and sign the Bill of Lading.

2.4 Pickup & Delivery Windows

All dates and times are estimates and may change due to weather, traffic, breakdowns, scheduling, or other factors outside OAT’s control. We are not liable for losses or expenses caused by delays.

2.5 Insurance & Liability

  • Vehicles moved on OAT-owned equipment are covered by our cargo and liability insurance as required by law.
  • If transport is arranged with a partner motor carrier, that carrier’s cargo and liability coverage will apply.
  • Coverage excludes pre-existing damage, normal wear and tear, mechanical issues, or items left in the vehicle.
  • For high-value or specialty vehicles, we recommend securing additional insurance coverage.

2.6 Damage Inspection & Claims

  • At pickup and delivery, the vehicle condition is recorded on the Bill of Lading.
  • Any new damage must be noted on the Bill of Lading at delivery and reported to OAT in writing within 24 hours.
  • OAT will assist in coordinating claims with the carrier’s insurance (if applicable), but payment responsibility lies with the carrier’s insurer.

2.7 Personal Items

Personal items are not permitted unless approved in writing. Any permitted items are transported at your own risk and may not be covered by insurance.
